A year ago I started documenting what I built — not polished tutorials, but raw process notes. What worked, what failed, what I thought at 2am debugging a pipeline.

## Why share the process

Final results impress for seconds. Processes create connection. When I share that a chunking strategy failed three times before working, other people feel less alone in their own bugs.

## The invisible cost

Writing while building has overhead. Sometimes it feels like doing two things halfway. But I realized **writing forces me to understand** — if I can't explain it, I didn't truly get it.

## What changed

- I get messages from people in similar situations
- My portfolio stopped being a project list and became a narrative
- I learned to communicate trade-offs — an underrated engineering skill

## An honest note

Not every day is inspiring. Some posts are just "today I broke production and fixed it." And that's fine. Authenticity > perfection.

If you're thinking about starting: don't wait for the perfect post. Publish today's note.
